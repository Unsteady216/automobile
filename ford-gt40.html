FORD GT40

"Built to Beat Ferrari"

View Specifications

Le Mans Domination

Born from Henry Ford II's desire to humble Enzo Ferrari, the GT40 was America's answer to European racing dominance. Standing just 40 inches tall (hence the name), this low-slung endurance racer was engineered with one goal: win Le Mans.

After initial struggles, the GT40 achieved immortality with four consecutive Le Mans victories from 1966 to 1969. The iconic 1-2-3 finish in 1966 remains one of motorsport's greatest moments, with three GT40s crossing the line together in a choreographed display of American engineering supremacy.

Powered by various Ford V8 engines including the legendary 7.0-liter unit, the GT40 combined American muscle with advanced aerodynamics and British craftsmanship to create the ultimate endurance racing weapon.

Racing Legacy

The GT40's dominance at Le Mans forever changed the landscape of endurance racing. Its victories proved that American engineering could compete with – and defeat – the best Europe had to offer. The car's success spawned a legacy that continues with modern Ford GT models.

Beyond Le Mans, the GT40 competed successfully in various international races, securing championships and establishing Ford's credentials as a serious motorsport manufacturer. Today, original GT40s are among the most valuable race cars in the world, with some examples selling for over $10 million.

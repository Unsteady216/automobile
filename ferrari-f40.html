FERRARI F40

"The Last Testament of Enzo"

View Specifications

Italian Supercar Perfection

The Ferrari F40, unveiled in 1987 to celebrate Ferrari's 40th anniversary, was Enzo Ferrari's final automotive statement before his death in 1988. It represented his pure vision: a street-legal race car with no compromises, no comfort features, just raw performance and passion.

Built around a tubular steel frame with Kevlar and carbon fiber body panels, the F40 weighed just 2,425 pounds. Its twin-turbocharged 2.9-liter V8 produced 478 horsepower, launching it to 60 mph in 3.8 seconds and on to a top speed of 201 mph – making it the first production car to break the 200 mph barrier.

Inside, there was no leather, no carpeting, no sound deadening – just exposed carbon fiber, racing seats, and the intoxicating sound of the V8 behind your head. The F40 wasn't meant to coddle; it was meant to thrill, and it succeeded brilliantly.

Supercar Icon

The F40 competed in various GT racing series throughout the late 1980s and early 1990s, proving that its performance capabilities extended beyond the street. Its race versions, particularly in IMSA competition, showcased Ferrari's racing DNA translated directly to a road car.

More than three decades after its introduction, the F40 remains the benchmark by which all supercars are measured. It represents a simpler time when driving experience mattered more than lap times or luxury features. Values have skyrocketed, with pristine examples now commanding prices well over $2 million, cementing its status as one of the greatest Ferraris ever built.

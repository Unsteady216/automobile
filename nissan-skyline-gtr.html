NISSAN SKYLINE GT-R R32

"Godzilla Awakens"

View Specifications

JDM Legend

The R32 Skyline GT-R, introduced in 1989, brought back the legendary GT-R nameplate after a 16-year hiatus. Nicknamed "Godzilla" by Australian racing media after its dominance in touring car racing, the R32 revolutionized performance car technology with its advanced ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system.

Powered by the legendary RB26DETT twin-turbocharged inline-six engine producing 280 horsepower (officially, but realistically closer to 320 HP), the R32 GT-R combined Japanese engineering excellence with raw performance. Its sophisticated AWD system could send up to 50% of power to the front wheels when needed, providing grip that was unheard of at the time.

The R32 won 29 consecutive races in Japanese Touring Car Championship from 1990 to 1993, cementing its legendary status. Today, it's the most affordable classic GT-R and has become an icon of JDM culture worldwide, especially after being featured in video games and movies.

Racing Dominance

The R32's racing success wasn't limited to Japan. In Australia's prestigious Bathurst 1000, the R32 shocked the motorsport world by defeating the dominant Ford and Holden V8 sedans. Its technology was so advanced that regulations were eventually changed to limit its dominance.

The RB26DETT engine became legendary in the tuning community, with enthusiasts regularly extracting 500-1000+ horsepower with modifications. This tuning potential, combined with the car's racing pedigree and iconic status, has made the R32 GT-R one of the most sought-after Japanese performance cars in history.

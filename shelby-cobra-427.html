SHELBY COBRA 427

"The Snake That Bit Back"

View Specifications

Raw Power Unleashed

The Shelby Cobra 427 represents the pinnacle of Carroll Shelby's vision: take a lightweight British AC Ace roadster and drop in Ford's monstrous 7.0-liter V8 engine. The result was automotive insanity – a car that could accelerate from 0-60 mph in under 4 seconds and reach speeds over 160 mph in 1965.

With its aggressive flared fenders, side exhausts, and purposeful stance, the 427 Cobra was built for one thing: domination. The car's aluminum body weighed just 2,400 pounds, giving it a power-to-weight ratio that embarrassed every supercar of its era and many that came after.

Only 348 427 Cobras were ever produced, making it one of the most valuable and sought-after American sports cars in history. Its raw, unfiltered driving experience – no power steering, no power brakes, just man and machine – remains legendary.

Racing Heritage

The Cobra 427 wasn't just a street car – it was a racing weapon. Carroll Shelby's team campaigned Cobras in various racing series throughout the 1960s, achieving remarkable success against European competitors with much larger budgets.

The 427's combination of American V8 power and nimble British chassis handling created a formula that couldn't be beaten. Its victories at tracks around the world cemented the Cobra's place in motorsport history and helped establish American performance credentials on the international stage.

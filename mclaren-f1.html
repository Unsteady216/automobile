MCLAREN F1

"Engineering Perfection"

View Specifications

The Greatest Supercar

The McLaren F1, unveiled in 1992, was the vision of legendary designer Gordon Murray: create the ultimate road car with no compromises. The result was a machine so advanced, so perfectly engineered, that it held the production car speed record for over a decade and remains one of the greatest automotive achievements in history.

The F1's most distinctive feature is its central driving position, flanked by two passenger seats. This unconventional layout provided the driver with perfect weight distribution and an unparalleled driving experience. The carbon fiber monocoque chassis weighed just 243 pounds, while the naturally aspirated BMW-derived 6.1-liter V12 produced 627 horsepower.

Only 106 F1s were built (64 road cars), making it one of the most exclusive supercars ever produced. In 1998, an F1 set the production car speed record at 240.1 mph – a record that stood until 2005. Today, F1s regularly sell for over $20 million, making them among the most valuable cars in the world.

Racing Glory

In an incredible demonstration of the F1's capabilities, a modified F1 GTR won the 1995 24 Hours of Le Mans outright on its first attempt – the last time a car designed primarily for road use would win the prestigious race. Five F1 GTRs finished in the top six, embarrassing purpose-built prototypes.

The F1's engineering philosophy – lightweight construction, naturally aspirated power, and manual transmission – stands in stark contrast to modern hypercars with their turbochargers, hybrid systems, and dual-clutch gearboxes. Many enthusiasts still consider the F1 the greatest driver's car ever made, a pure expression of automotive perfection that may never be equaled.

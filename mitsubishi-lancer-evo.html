MITSUBISHI LANCER EVOLUTION VI TME

Rally Heritage

The Tommi MÃ¤kinen Edition (TME) of the Lancer Evolution VI, released in 1999, honored the Finnish rally legend who won four consecutive World Rally Championships with Mitsubishi from 1996-1999. This special edition combined championship-winning technology with enhancements that made it the ultimate Evo.

The TME featured a unique front bumper for improved aerodynamics, titanium turbine wheels that were lighter than standard, Recaro bucket seats, and a distinctive red/silver paint scheme. The 2.0-liter turbocharged 4G63 engine produced 280 horsepower (Japan's gentlemen's agreement figure), but the true output was closer to 300 HP.

What made the Evolution special was its sophisticated all-wheel-drive system with Active Yaw Control (AYC), which could vector torque between the rear wheels for incredible cornering ability. This technology, derived directly from rally competition, gave the Evo handling that rivaled supercars costing many times more.

WRC Dominance

The Evolution series was born from Mitsubishi's World Rally Championship program, and the direct connection between race car and road car was stronger than almost any other manufacturer. Technologies developed on gravel stages around the world found their way directly into production Evos, creating a true rally car for the street.

The Evo VI TME represents the peak of the "golden era" of Japanese performance cars. Its combination of turbocharged power, advanced AWD technology, and raw driving dynamics made it a giant killer on both road and track. Today, clean TME examples are highly sought after by collectors, with values appreciating significantly as enthusiasts recognize the Evo VI as one of the greatest performance bargains in automotive history.

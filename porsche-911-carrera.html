PORSCHE 911 CARRERA RS 2.7

"The Ultimate Air-Cooled Icon"

View Specifications

German Precision Engineering

The 1973 Porsche 911 Carrera RS 2.7 represents the pinnacle of air-cooled 911 design. Created to homologate the 911 for FIA Group 4 racing, Porsche produced what many consider the greatest sports car ever made. The iconic ducktail spoiler wasn't just for looks â€“ it provided crucial high-speed stability.

Weighing just 2,370 pounds thanks to thinner steel body panels and lightweight components, the RS 2.7 combined a larger 2.7-liter flat-six engine with precision handling that set new benchmarks for driver engagement. Every aspect was optimized for pure driving pleasure.

Originally planned for 500 units, demand was so overwhelming that Porsche produced 1,580 examples. Today, the RS 2.7 is one of the most coveted and valuable classic Porsches, with pristine examples commanding seven-figure prices.

Racing Pedigree

The Carrera RS dominated touring car racing throughout the 1970s, winning championships across Europe. Its lightweight construction and powerful engine made it nearly unbeatable in its class, establishing Porsche's reputation for building race-winning production cars.

The RS's influence extends far beyond its racing success. It established the formula for all subsequent high-performance 911s: lightweight construction, powerful naturally aspirated engine, and suspension tuned for maximum driver feedback. Every RS, GT3, and GT2 that followed owes its existence to this 1973 masterpiece.
